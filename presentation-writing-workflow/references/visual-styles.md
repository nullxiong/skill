# Visual Styles

## Internal Sharing

- Default style: cool, cinematic, high-impact, story-first.
- Use dark or high-contrast compositions when appropriate.
- Favor big titles, atmospheric backgrounds, strong depth, dramatic lighting, chapter rhythm, and reveal moments.
- Avoid dense tables and management-report language unless requested.

## Internal Reporting

- Default style: restrained, structured, readable, executive.
- Favor white or neutral backgrounds, clear grids, KPI cards, decision boxes, timelines, and risk matrices.
- Use color to encode meaning, not decoration.
- Avoid overly cinematic visuals that hide the message.

## Customer Briefing

- Default style: professional, clean, premium, moderately technological.
- Favor modern layouts, elegant spacing, subtle technical textures, architecture diagrams, capability maps, and proof points.
- Use technology cues with restraint: grids, light lines, data traces, soft glow, precise diagrams.
- Avoid excessive sci-fi styling, heavy darkness, or visual effects that reduce trust.

## Visual Intensity Scale

- Low: functional, clean, highly readable.
- Medium: polished, branded, modern, some visual depth.
- High: cinematic, dramatic, immersive, strong emotional impact.

Use high intensity mainly for internal sharing, opening pages, chapter dividers, key turning points, or closing pages.
